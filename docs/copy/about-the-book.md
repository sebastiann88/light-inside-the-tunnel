### Opening Hook

There's a moment — maybe it's 3 AM, maybe it's mid-afternoon in a meeting you don't care about — when you realize you've been living someone else's version of your life. Not dramatically. Not in a way that makes a good movie. Just quietly, consistently, one small compromise at a time.

This book is about that moment. And what comes after.

---

### Synopsis

*The Light Inside the Tunnel* doesn't offer a five-step plan or a morning routine. It offers something harder and more honest: a way of thinking about who you are, based not on what you believe in theory, but on what you actually do in practice.

At its core is **Consistencialism** — the idea that your real philosophy isn't what you say at dinner parties or post online. It's the one that shows up in your hands, your habits, your Tuesday afternoons. The beliefs that reach your actions are the only beliefs that matter.

The book moves through questions most of us carry but rarely examine out loud. What does it mean to be consistent — not rigid, but genuinely aligned with yourself? Why is it so hard to practice what we know? And what happens when we stop pretending that intention is enough?

It's written in plain language, with warmth and occasional humor, by someone who's been in the tunnel and isn't pretending he's found all the exits.

---

### Who Is This Book For?

If you've ever felt like the life you're living doesn't quite match the one you meant to build — this is for you.

If you've read philosophy before and found it cold, abstract, or disconnected from the mess of being human — this is for you.

If you've never read philosophy at all but you think about things at 3 AM — this is especially for you.

This isn't a book for academics. It's a book for anyone with a heart and a few honest questions.
