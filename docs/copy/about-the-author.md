### The Story

I didn't set out to write a philosophy book. I set out to answer a question that wouldn't leave me alone: *Why do I keep not doing the things I say matter most to me?*

That question followed me for years — through late nights, through work that was good but not quite right, through the slow realization that knowing something and living it are two very different things.

So I started writing. Not to teach anyone anything — I hadn't earned that — but to think out loud, honestly, about what it means to live consistently with who you actually are. Not who you intend to be. Not who you were. Who you are, right now, measured by what you actually do.

The result is *The Light Inside the Tunnel*. It's the book I needed to read before I could write it.

### Design Meets Philosophy

By day, I'm a graphic designer. I run [Skylight Designs](https://skylightdesigns.ca) here in Ottawa, where I help people communicate clearly through visual design.

It turns out that design and philosophy have more in common than you'd think. Both are about clarity. Both demand that you strip away what doesn't serve the work. And both ask the same uncomfortable question: *Is this honest?*

This book was designed with the same care I bring to every project — because how something is made should reflect what it means.
