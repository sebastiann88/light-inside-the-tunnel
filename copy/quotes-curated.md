## CURATION NOTES

- **FINAL: 27 Sebastian originals, 2 collaborations/attributions (Rodrigue Nitounga, Gandhi), 1 from chapter teaser copy = 30 total.** The page is overwhelmingly Sebastian's voice.
- **Three batches curated.** Batch 1 (100+ quotes): initial 30. Batch 2: added "Seek to understand where the person shines," "The smallest light can bright up your spirit," swapped in "Quiet deeds speak the loudest words." Batch 3: added "Gifts do not replace the human presence" (Nitounga), "The world becomes lighter, when your thoughts are not so heavy," "Keep your thoughts deep, and your mood light."
- **Dropped across all batches:** "True fulfillment comes from peace, virtue, and harmony" (generic), "Success is relative to each individual" (generic), "Conflict is not the enemy. Indifference is." (echoed #15 on justice/indifference), "Character is formed through virtue..." (didn't surprise), Nietzsche quote (freed a spot for Sebastian's own words).
- **Quotes #18 and #20** were drawn from chapter teaser copy I wrote, not directly from Sebastian's quote collection. They should be verified against the actual book text before publishing. If they're not in the book, cut them — we're still at 28.
- **#19 (Rodrigue Nitounga)** — "Gifts do not replace the human presence." The one non-Sebastian, non-Gandhi quote. Five words, profound. Nitounga is a collaborator referenced throughout Sebastian's collection — including one attribution honors that relationship.
- **#23 is the full "To have fun" quote.** The longer version gives the punchline more weight. It lands harder because of the setup.
- **#13 (compassion) is the longest quote.** It earns its length — "when they are wounded, you cry" is one of the most emotionally powerful lines in the collection. The designer should typeset this one with care.
- **#16 ("different lyrics, same instrumental")** is the most poetic and unexpected quote in the set. Strong social media performance potential.
- **#22 ("The world becomes lighter") and #24 ("The smallest light")** both tie to the book title's "light" metaphor — but through different angles (weightlessness vs. illumination). They complement rather than duplicate.
- **Groupings are for curation reference only** — the Quotes page itself should not display category headers. Quotes should flow freely in the masonry grid, mixing themes.
- **No duplicate ideas.** Across all three batches, the strongest version of each idea was kept and all variations cut.
- **The "5-second test":** Every quote on this list was evaluated for standalone impact. Can someone read it cold, out of context, and feel something? If not, it didn't make the cut.

## QUOTES NOT INCLUDED (and why)

- Quotes that required paragraph-length context to land — the page is for standalone moments
- Attributed quotes beyond the 2 selected — the page should be Sebastian's voice
- Variations/duplicates of included quotes — strongest version kept
- Quotes that felt inspirational-poster generic without the book's context
- Anything that read as advice rather than observation — the book earns the right to advise; a quote page doesn't
- Coaching-style quotes (Reid Ivens collaboration) — good principles but need context to land as standalone moments
- Mixed metaphors or lines that required a second read to parse
- Quotes where the sentiment was true but the phrasing didn't surprise ("Authenticity allows us to remove our personas" — clean but expected)
- Religious framing that might narrow the audience on a public page
