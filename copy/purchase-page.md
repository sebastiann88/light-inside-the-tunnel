## COPY NOTES

- **Direct sales changes the energy.** Buying directly from the author is more personal than clicking through to Amazon. The copy leans into that: "directly from Sebastian. No middlemen." This isn't a workaround — it's a feature. It fits the book's philosophy of directness and human connection.
- **"Order your copy" instead of "Buy now."** Still warm, but "order" has more weight than "get" for a direct purchase action. It implies intent without pressure. We avoid "Buy now" — too transactional for this site.
- **Price is displayed.** Unlike the multi-retailer version, a direct sales page should show the price. No surprises. Honesty is on-brand.
- **This page is still deliberately minimal.** The reader has already decided. One sentence of context, the format options, and a button. The book cover and pull quote do the rest.
- **The intro line does double work.** "No middlemen, no algorithms, no massive retailer between you and the person who wrote it" — this justifies the direct model while reinforcing the book's values. Connection without intermediaries. Practice, not performance.
- **Developer note:** The Purchase Links global set may need restructuring for direct sales — instead of retailer name + external URL, it may need format, price, and an on-site purchase flow (Craft Commerce, Snipcart, or simple Stripe integration). Flagging for the developer.
