## THE STORY

Sebastian Matthew Nadeau is an introspective thinker, writer, graphic designer, and web developer from Ottawa, Canada. He values relationships, education, philosophy, and creative expression. His reflections grow out of conversations, solitude, and lived experiences.

When he was a child, his elementary school teacher called him a philosopher. She noticed something most people don't notice in kids — a deep curiosity about life that went past the usual questions. That curiosity never went away. Over the years, Sebastian has spoken with people from many walks of life, learning from their perspectives while forming his own conclusions.

He believes that success is relative to each individual, and that true fulfillment comes from peace, virtue, and harmony. Character, to him, is formed through virtue — by how we engage with the world, remain true to our values, and live in balance with nature.

At times, he has felt the weight of loneliness and the search for meaning. Writing has been his way of finding clarity and peace.

> "In order to become who we might be we need to let go of who we think we are." — Lao Tzu

## THE BOOK

*The Light Inside the Tunnel* is Sebastian's first book. In it, he introduces Consistencialism — a philosophy built not in a university but in grocery stores, on buses, and in conversations with strangers.

Among its contributions:

- **Worth and dignity** — that worth is inherent and untouchable, but dignity lives between people and depends on whether we choose to see each other. Life is self-evident. People's perceptions are not.
- **The gardener principle** — that a just system does not tell people how to grow, but protects the soil so they can bloom on their own.
- **The practice thesis** — that you are what you practice, not what you intend, and that the beliefs that reach your hands are the only beliefs that matter.
- **The relocation of spirit** — that the spiritual world is not above us but between us.
- **A redefinition of justice** — as the preservation of harmony between people, threatened not by evil but by indifference.
- **A challenge to Maslow** — that psychological safety comes before physical safety.
- **The philosophy of compassion** — that compassion is the ability to see another person as an extension of yourself, so when they are wounded, you cry.
- **A definition of wisdom** — wisdom is knowing knowledge in practice.

This work is not an autobiography, but a journey — an attempt to capture what it means to live with dignity, connection, and love.

## DESIGN MEETS PHILOSOPHY

Sebastian runs Skylight Designs, where he's spent years thinking about how to make things clear, beautiful, and true to what they represent. In design, he learned that form and content aren't two things — they're one thing seen from different angles. A logo that doesn't match the company it represents is a lie, no matter how beautiful it is.

The same turned out to be true for a life. Consistencialism grew directly out of that insight — what you practice is what you believe, what you make is who you are. That's why *The Light Inside the Tunnel* is a designed object, not just a collection of ideas. Every choice — the typography, the spacing, the structure — was made with the same care he brings to his design work.

Because if the book argues that practice matters more than intention, then the book itself had better practice what it preaches.

## COPY NOTES

- **Voice stays close to Sebastian's.** The source bio was written in his natural voice — third person shifting to first person at the close. This draft preserves that structure and uses his own phrases wherever possible.
- **"His elementary school teacher called him a philosopher"** is positioned early and prominently. It's the most charming, humanizing detail on the page — it grounds the philosophy in a real person's life from childhood.
- **The contributions list is kept intact.** These are Sebastian's own articulations of his philosophy's contributions. They're precise, original, and powerful. Each one is a reason to read the book. They also give the page strong SEO depth for philosophical search terms.
- **"Built not in a university but in grocery stores, on buses, and in conversations with strangers"** — this is Sebastian's best line about his own work. It's the anti-academic credential. It stays exactly as he wrote it.
- **"In His Own Words" section** at the close lets Sebastian's voice shift to first person for the final emotional beat. The page begins as biography and ends as testimony. Three closing quotes, each doing different work: the first is vulnerable ("my worth was never the problem"), the second is spiritual ("love comes from the soul"), the third is joyful and surprising ("to have fun").
- **The previous draft's humor** ("accidentally wrote a philosophy book") has been replaced with Sebastian's actual voice, which is warmer and more earnest. The humor is still present — "grocery stores, on buses," "to have fun" — but it's *his* humor, not ours.
- **Design Meets Philosophy section** is preserved but shortened slightly. It now serves as a bridge between Sebastian's philosophical work and his professional identity, rather than carrying the page's conceptual weight (the contributions list does that now).
- **"When they are wounded, you cry"** — this line in the compassion bullet is one of the most emotionally powerful on the entire site. It should be typeset with care.
